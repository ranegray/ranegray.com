an undergraduate student starting a robotics lab seems a little bit weird. but if i don't start it now, when would i?

RGRL is the rane gray robotics lab. an indie research lab. it's early. right now it's just me, a small apartment in Boulder, and a handful of robots. but the idea is bigger than the space.

i want to expand my knowledge of robotics and do it in a way that's open and collaborative. not behind closed doors in some corporate lab, but out in the open where anyone can follow along, ask questions, and eventually contribute.

there's this stigma that robotics is hard to get into. and humanoids specifically are capital intensive. you need serious money and serious hardware. but i think we can change that. we need ways to skill people up so they can work with these systems themselves. stream it. teach it. build kits. partner with schools. i don't have all the answers yet, but the direction is clear: put the tools in people's hands.

the logistics of actually running a lab? i don't know a ton about that yet. but i intend to learn, and i intend to learn in public.

what will RGRL become? ideally, a place where anyone can come and learn about robotics. a place to build things, share ideas, and collaborate. together we can democratize humanoid robotics and capture value for the people, not just the companies.

it's early. but it's started.
